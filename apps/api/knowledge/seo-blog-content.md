# SEO + blog content — the compounding distribution surface

Blog content is the only distribution surface that compounds for free over years. A YouTube video peaks in 30-90 days. A Reddit post is forgotten in a week. A well-ranked blog post drives traffic for 24-60 months and the cost has already been paid. Every operator business should have a blog by month 3 — not for vanity, but because it's the cheapest durable acquisition channel that exists.

## What the operator should remember

- Google still dominates discovery for high-intent commerce search. AI search (Perplexity, ChatGPT search, Google AIO) is shifting some of this but rewards the same underlying signals: authority + relevance + freshness.
- Rankings compound nonlinearly. The first 6 months of a new blog look like nothing. Months 6-18 look like everything.
- A blog post that ranks #3 for a buyer-intent keyword can be worth more than 100 social posts that briefly trend.
- The brain should NEVER spam thin AI-generated content. Google's helpful-content system (HCU) detects and penalizes it; recovery from an HCU hit takes 6-12 months and may never fully reverse.

## Keyword research — the only kind that matters

Stop hunting for "high search volume". Hunt for **buyer intent matched to your offer**. A keyword with 100 searches/month where 5% become customers beats a keyword with 100,000 searches/month where 0.001% do.

Three intent categories the brain weighs differently:

1. **Buy-intent** — "[product] review", "best [category] for [use case]", "[brand A] vs [brand B]", "where to buy [thing]". HIGHEST priority. Conversion-ready traffic. Operator should produce these first.
2. **Solution-intent** — "how to [solve problem]", "[problem] fix", "why is my [thing] [bad outcome]". MEDIUM priority. Top-of-funnel but trust-building. Pair with a lead magnet.
3. **Information-intent** — "what is [concept]", "[topic] explained", "history of [topic]". LOW priority unless the niche IS education / info-products. Long-tail expansion only.

The brain's keyword pipeline:
- Start with 3-5 seed terms describing the operator's niche
- Expand via the operator's existing audience signal (search-console queries, Reddit subreddit titles, YouTube comments)
- Score each candidate by (intent × search volume × current ranking difficulty)
- Reject anything with current top-10 dominated by sites with DR 70+ unless operator has clear topical-authority moat
- Output: 20-50 prioritized keywords ranked by expected conversion-weighted traffic

## Content structure — what ranks in 2026

A blog post that ranks today has 4 traits, in order of importance:

1. **Answers the specific question fast.** Above-the-fold answer in the first 100 words. Don't bury the lede. Google reads the first 1-2 paragraphs as the canonical answer and surfaces them in AI Overviews.
2. **Demonstrates first-hand experience.** Real screenshots, real numbers from real attempts, real failures the operator actually encountered. The HCU system specifically rewards "evidence the author has done the thing".
3. **Comprehensive without padding.** Cover the topic exhaustively for someone arriving with the query, but cut filler. 1,500 dense words beats 3,000 padded ones. Optimal length varies by query type; the brain checks top-10 ranking pages for current word-count distribution.
4. **Internal linking to and from authoritative pages on the same site.** A new post linked from your highest-ranking existing post inherits some of its authority. Reciprocally, the new post links to your offer page.

## On-page checklist the brain enforces

- Title tag ≤ 60 chars, includes the primary keyword, leads with the value not the brand
- H1 matches the title in intent, may differ in wording for variety
- URL slug = primary keyword, hyphenated, no stop words
- Meta description 140-160 chars, includes a benefit + a soft call-to-action
- First paragraph contains the primary keyword in the first sentence, ideally as a natural question/answer
- At least 3 internal links to related posts on the same site
- At least 1 link to an external high-authority source (.gov, .edu, or top-10 industry source) as evidence
- Schema markup: Article + Product (if applicable) + FAQPage (if Q&A format)
- One H2 every 200-300 words for scannability
- Featured image with alt text describing the topic
- Last-updated date visible — freshness signal

## The 6-12-24 cadence

The single biggest mistake new blogs make is publishing 1 post and quitting. Search algorithms reward sustained publishing:

- **Months 0-3 (foundation):** 6 cornerstone posts. Each targets a primary buy-intent keyword. Each is 1,500-3,000 words. Each is heavily internal-linked to your offer. No daily posts yet — quality over cadence.
- **Months 3-12 (expansion):** Add 1-2 posts per week. Mix buy-intent, solution-intent, and timely commentary on niche news. Each post links to relevant cornerstone posts.
- **Months 12-24 (compound):** Maintain 1 post per week. Refresh older cornerstone posts every 4-6 months. Watch for cannibalization — two posts ranking for the same query split traffic and hurt both.

The brain auto-schedules this cadence per business via `schedule.save`. If volume drops below 1 post/week for 30 days, the briefing surfaces it.

## Refresh > new posts after month 12

A cornerstone post from month 3 that ranks #8 is often easier to push to #4 than to write a new post that ranks at all. The brain's content audit (quarterly) should:

1. Identify top 20 posts by impressions (Search Console)
2. Filter to those ranking positions 4-15 (the climbable range)
3. Refresh: update intro, expand thin sections, add 2-3 new internal links, swap old screenshots, update the published-date metadata
4. Re-promote (email blast + social) on the refresh

A single quarterly refresh of 5 climbing posts often yields more new traffic than 12 brand-new posts at the same effort.

## What the brain MUST NOT do

- Auto-publish AI-generated content without operator review. The HCU penalty is real and recovery is brutal.
- Stuff keywords. The 2010 keyword-density advice is actively harmful in 2026. Write naturally.
- Buy backlinks or participate in PBN networks. Same risk profile as buying followers, but multi-year recovery instead of months.
- Cloak content (show different content to Google than to users). Manual penalty class, very hard to recover.
- Copy competitor content. Even "AI rephrasing" is detectable.
- Publish on a brand-new domain expecting traffic in month 1. Set the operator's expectation: 6 months to first meaningful traffic.

## Cost economics

At small scale, a 50-post blog targeting 50 commercial keywords with reasonable execution yields:

- Months 0-6: ~0 organic traffic, 5-15 indexed posts
- Months 6-12: 500-3,000 monthly organic visits as long-tail begins ranking
- Months 12-24: 3,000-15,000 monthly organic visits as cornerstones climb
- Months 24+: 10,000-50,000+ monthly organic visits with continued cadence

At a 1-3% conversion rate from organic traffic to email-list opt-in, that's 100-1,500 new subscribers per month for free at maturity. The compounding is why the brain prioritizes this even though it takes 6 months to show any signal.

## Integration with the rest of the stack

- **Mailchimp / ConvertKit / Beehiiv** — every blog post has a lead magnet specific to the topic. List growth IS the success metric for content marketing, more than pageviews.
- **YouTube / Pinterest** — repurpose blog posts as video scripts + pinnable graphics. One blog post → 3-5 derivative pieces of content for free distribution.
- **Shopify / Printful / Gumroad** — every post links to the relevant offer page. Affiliate links (for non-owned products) only AFTER first-party offer links. Track conversion attribution via UTM parameters.
- **Reddit / X** — share each post once in 2-3 highly relevant communities. Never spam. The 9:1 ratio in the Reddit playbook applies.

The brain's content calendar is one of the canonical planning surfaces; the briefing surfaces "next 5 posts to write, next 3 to refresh, next 1 to deprecate" every Monday.
